<h2>Key Information</h2>
<h3>Learning Through Activities</h3>
<p>In Hong Wen School, students learn Mathematical concepts and skills through
the Concrete-Pictorial-Abstract approach. Through hands-on activities and
the use of manipulatives, students are able to see abstract mathematical
concepts in concrete and pictorial form, which leads to deeper conceptual
understanding of the Mathematical concepts and skills taught.&nbsp; Opportunities
are also provided for students to build their reasoning and communication
skills in class, whether it is through in-class participation or individual
journaling tasks.</p>

<h3>Factual Fluency</h3>
<p>Our students go through daily exercises to build their factual fluency,
including but not limited to their number bonds from 1 to 20, and multiplication
facts.&nbsp; Having good factual fluency builds studentsâ€™ confidence and&nbsp;
contributes to their ability to solve mathematical problems.</p>
<h3>P5 Mathaquarium</h3>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">
<img style="width: 100%" height="auto" width="100%" alt="math-aquarium" src="https://lh6.googleusercontent.com/o_wRUobuSaq0c4lNzSAAgVvdH5QUQqo9hDRGoQml0sky2JP7S2KN5ewSO_Fb_-h7KDGifY2cF6sT5VQVNEOFUkpJzy1C4xSWhPtFUiz-Q6iXTpM96ev9XUTcHuD1boz5JlwpOm2Uw-ABxl6TRsbY3deYZ6VFTWK8">
</div>
<p>The objective of the programme is for all Primary 5 students to work in
groups and to express their creativity and innovative thinking through
game design. Students have to come out with their own game concept and
infuse Mathematics in the games. On Mathaquarium Day, all projects are
exhibited for the rest of the school to enjoy playing and learning.</p>
